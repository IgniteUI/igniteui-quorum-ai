Duplication:
- Do NOT propose any step that is semantically equivalent to an existing step, even if worded differently. Two steps are duplicates if they would produce the same outcome when executed
- Steps that follow the same pattern but target different fields are NEVER duplicates, even when proposed in the same batch. E.g. "Determine the success color for a bright Christmas theme" and "Determine the error color for a bright Christmas theme" modify different fields (success color vs error color) and are both valid. Do NOT treat similarly worded steps as duplicates when they operate on different fields
- Before proposing a step, verify it does not overlap with any existing step's intent or effect
Logic:
- CRITICAL: Each step MUST modify exactly ONE thing — one field, one value, one concept. No exceptions. If a step touches more than one field or concept, it MUST be split into separate steps
- A step that modifies two or more fields is ALWAYS wrong, even if the fields are related
- VIOLATION EXAMPLES (never do these):
  - "Determine the name and the age" → WRONG, split into two steps
  - "Calculate width, height, and depth" → WRONG, split into three steps
  - "Determine the X and generate the Y" → WRONG, split into two steps
  - "Validate X against Y and Z and adjust accordingly" → WRONG, validates against two things — split into two steps
- "Validate and adjust" IS allowed when targeting a single field/concept, because pure validation without a state change is not a valid step. The adjustment is the state change that makes the step actionable
- Do not split generating a value and setting it into separate steps
- The steps should be simple to execute, atomic, maximally decomposed, incremental, and quick to execute by a single agent
- The steps should require minimal context to execute
- The steps should directly contribute to completing the overall task and be actionable without requiring further decomposition
- The result of the steps should provide output and be executable by an AI agent
- Do not give examples or suggestions. Only concrete actionable steps
- Do not include justifications or require such in the steps
- Do not suggest steps which set something to a concrete value. Values will be determined during execution
- Do NOT suggest setting a field to something. Instead, suggest determining a value for the field
- Steps should describe WHAT to determine or transform, not HOW
